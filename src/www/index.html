A Brief History


The concept of extroversion and introversion is probably the oldest beliefs in the history of personality theory as it dates back to the 1920s with the psychologist Carl Jung. Carl Jung believes there are two mutually exclusive attitudes: extraversion and introversion – “each person seems to be energized more by either the external world (extraversion) or the internal world (introversion).”
Today, many individuals are able to complete personality tests such as the popular Myers-Briggs Type Indicator to determine whether one is an introvert or extrovert.



Are You an Introvert?


You enjoy spending quality time alone and would rather have a quiet intimate conversation with someone than a loud group conversation. You are someone who dreads situations where you must interact with others for a long period of time, and you listen to music in public mostly so that you can avoid any sorts of interaction. If most of these are true, then you are likely an introvert.
Introverts are not necessarily shy as shy people are anxious or panicked in social events; introverts are people who just find other people tiring.
